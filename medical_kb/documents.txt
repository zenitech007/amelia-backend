TITLE: Peptic Ulcer Disease (PUD)
CATEGORY: Gastroenterology

OVERVIEW:
Peptic ulcer disease occurs when the protective lining of the stomach or duodenum becomes damaged, often due to Helicobacter pylori infection or chronic NSAID use.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
Burning stomach pain
Pain worse on empty stomach
Bloating
Nausea
Loss of appetite

DIET GUIDANCE (NIGERIA):
Avoid heavily spiced foods such as pepper soup, suya, and raw scotch bonnet pepper (ata rodo).
Avoid excessive palm oil and alcohol.

RECOMMENDED MANAGEMENT:
Use antacids or Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) such as omeprazole under medical supervision.

MEDICATION WARNINGS:
Avoid NSAIDs such as ibuprofen or diclofenac because they worsen gastric ulcers.

CLINICAL NOTE:
Untreated ulcers can lead to bleeding or stomach perforation.

REGION: Nigeria

TITLE: Hypertension Lifestyle Management
CATEGORY: Cardiovascular Medicine

OVERVIEW:
Hypertension is highly prevalent in West Africa and is a major risk factor for stroke, kidney disease, and heart failure.

SYMPTOMS:
Often asymptomatic
Headaches
Dizziness
Blurred vision

DIET GUIDANCE:
Reduce sodium intake.

COMMON NIGERIAN HIGH-SODIUM SOURCES:
Bouillon cubes (Maggi, Knorr)
Salted meats
Processed foods

RECOMMENDED FOODS:
Bananas
Avocados
Leafy vegetables
Beans

CLINICAL GUIDANCE:
Encourage regular blood pressure monitoring.

RISK THRESHOLD:
Blood pressure above 130/80 mmHg is considered elevated.

REGION: West Africa

TITLE: Type 2 Diabetes Management
CATEGORY: Endocrinology

OVERVIEW:
Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body cannot effectively regulate blood glucose levels.

SYMPTOMS:
Frequent urination
Excessive thirst
Fatigue
Blurred vision
Slow wound healing

HIGH GLYCEMIC FOODS COMMON IN NIGERIA:
Polished white rice
Cassava-based foods such as fufu or eba
Sugary drinks

LOWER GLYCEMIC ALTERNATIVES:
Unripe plantain flour
Guinea corn (sorghum)
Millet
Fonio (acha)
Ofada rice

CLINICAL GUIDANCE:
Emphasize portion control and regular fasting blood glucose monitoring.

RISK:
Poorly controlled diabetes may cause kidney failure, nerve damage, and blindness.

REGION: Nigeria

TITLE: Malaria Clinical Presentation
CATEGORY: Infectious Disease

OVERVIEW:
Malaria is a mosquito-borne parasitic infection common in tropical regions.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
High fever
Chills
Sweating
Headache
Body pain
Fatigue

DIAGNOSTIC TESTS:
Malaria rapid diagnostic test
Microscopy blood film

CLINICAL NOTE:
Symptoms may resemble viral infections or typhoid fever.

PREVENTION:
Sleep under insecticide-treated nets
Remove stagnant water near homes

REGION: Sub-Saharan Africa

TITLE: Typhoid Fever
CATEGORY: Infectious Disease

OVERVIEW:
Typhoid fever is caused by Salmonella typhi bacteria transmitted through contaminated food or water.

SYMPTOMS:
Persistent fever
Abdominal pain
Weakness
Diarrhea or constipation
Loss of appetite

DIAGNOSTIC TESTS:
Blood culture
Stool culture
Widal test

MEDICAL WARNING:
Do not self-medicate with antibiotics without laboratory confirmation.

REGION: Nigeria

TITLE: Lassa Fever Emergency Protocol
CATEGORY: Viral Hemorrhagic Fever

OVERVIEW:
Lassa fever is a viral hemorrhagic illness transmitted through exposure to infected rodents.

EARLY SYMPTOMS:
Fever
Weakness
Headache

SEVERE SYMPTOMS:
Facial swelling
Bleeding
Shock

ACTION:
Immediate hospital isolation required.

REPORTING:
Contact the Nigeria Centre for Disease Control (NCDC).

EMERGENCY HOTLINE:
6232

TITLE: Cholera Outbreak Response
CATEGORY: Infectious Disease

OVERVIEW:
Cholera is a severe bacterial infection causing profuse watery diarrhea.

SYMPTOMS:
Rice-water stool
Vomiting
Rapid dehydration
Weakness

IMMEDIATE RESPONSE:
Administer Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS).

SEVERE CASES:
Require intravenous fluids at a hospital.

PREVENTION:
Drink only boiled or treated water.

REGION: West Africa

TITLE: Tuberculosis Detection
CATEGORY: Respiratory Disease

OVERVIEW:
Tuberculosis is a contagious bacterial infection affecting the lungs.

KEY SYMPTOMS:
Cough lasting longer than 2–3 weeks
Night sweats
Weight loss
Blood in sputum

TREATMENT:
Directly Observed Treatment Short-course (DOTS) lasting six months.

NOTE:
Treatment is available free at many Nigerian government clinics.

REGION: Nigeria

TITLE: Snakebite Emergency First Aid
CATEGORY: Emergency Medicine

OVERVIEW:
Snakebite envenomation can be life-threatening and requires urgent hospital treatment.

FIRST RESPONSE:
Keep patient calm and still.
Keep affected limb below heart level.

DO NOT:
Cut the wound
Attempt to suck out venom
Apply tight tourniquets

ACTION:
Transport immediately to hospital for antivenom treatment.

TITLE: Sickle Cell Disease (SS Genotype)
CATEGORY: Hematology

OVERVIEW:
Sickle cell disease is a genetic blood disorder causing red blood cells to become rigid and sickle-shaped.

COMMON COMPLICATIONS:
Pain crises
Severe anemia
Infections

TRIGGERS:
Dehydration
Malaria
Extreme temperatures
Stress

DAILY MANAGEMENT:
Drink 3–4 liters of water
Take folic acid supplementation
Prevent malaria infections

REGION: Sub-Saharan Africa

TITLE: Asthma Management During Harmattan
CATEGORY: Respiratory Disease

OVERVIEW:
Dust and dry air during the Harmattan season can trigger asthma attacks.

SYMPTOMS:
Wheezing
Shortness of breath
Chest tightness
Coughing

PREVENTION:
Use inhaled corticosteroid preventers daily.
Carry a reliever inhaler such as salbutamol.

ADDITIONAL PROTECTION:
Wear masks outdoors.
Stay hydrated.

REGION: West Africa

TITLE: Oral Rehydration Therapy
CATEGORY: Gastroenterology

OVERVIEW:
Diarrhea and vomiting can rapidly cause dehydration in tropical climates.

FIRST-LINE RESPONSE:
Administer Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS).

PREPARATION:
Mix ORS with clean drinking water.

ADDITIONAL SUPPORT:
Zinc supplementation improves recovery.
